Security controls

A sample of the controls tracked in our internal SOC 2 checklist.

Implemented

AES-256-GCM encryption at rest

Database connection secrets, SAML private keys, and API keys are encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a dedicated ENCRYPTION_KEY, never the JWT secret.

Implemented

TLS 1.2+ in transit

All customer-database connections use strict TLS by default. Self-signed certs are rejected in production unless explicitly opted-in.

Implemented

JWT session auth with audience & issuer

Signed JWTs with short expiry, rotation, and audience/issuer claims. bcrypt for password hashing with per-user salt.

Implemented

Custom Semgrep + Gitleaks rules

Every commit is scanned for secret leakage and for our application-specific anti-patterns before it can merge.

Implemented

Principle of least privilege

Workspace-scoped RBAC (owner / admin / editor / viewer). Superadmin access is gated by separate controls and audit-logged.

Rolling out

SAML 2.0 SSO & SCIM 2.0

Okta, Entra ID, Google Workspace. SCIM provisioning for automated user and group sync. Available in the Business tier.

Rolling out

IP allow-listing

Workspace-level CIDR allow-lists enforced before authentication. Available in the Business tier.

Rolling out

Immutable audit log export

Export every control-plane event to your S3-compatible bucket or SIEM in near-real-time. Available in the Enterprise tier.

Privacy

Data minimization
We collect only what is needed to render the dashboard and alert on your databases. Query text is optional and user-controlled.
Sub-processors
Neon (Postgres hosting, AWS Singapore), Vercel (edge + serverless), Railway/Fly.io (long-lived backend), AWS (object storage). Full list at /sub-processors.
Data residency
Default region is AWS Singapore. US and EU regions are available on the Business and Enterprise tiers.
Data deletion
Customer data is deleted within 30 days of account termination. Documented procedure and attestation on request.
